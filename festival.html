Gion Matsuri

Gion Matsuri is a month-long festival in Kyoto, Japan, held annually in July, and is one of Japan's most famous festivals. It's centered around the Yasaka Shrine and features elaborate floats, traditional music, and various events. The festival's history dates back to 869, when it began as a purification ritual to appease gods and ward off epidemics.

Aoi Matsuri

The Aoi Matsuri, also known as the Hollyhock Festival, is one of Kyoto's three most famous festivals, held annually on May 15th. It is a vibrant display of ancient traditions, featuring a grand procession of over 500 people in Heian-era costumes, including oxcarts, horses, and a Saio-dai maiden, making their way from the Kyoto Imperial Palace to Shimogamo and Kamigamo Shrines. The festival is named for the hollyhock (aoi) leaves that adorn the participants and decorations.

Jidai Matsuri

Jidai Matsuri, also known as the Festival of the Ages, is a Japanese festival held annually on October 22nd in Kyoto, Japan. It is one of Kyoto's three major festivals, alongside the Aoi Matsuri and the Gion Matsuri. The festival is a grand procession featuring participants in elaborate costumes representing various periods of Japanese history, from the Heian period to the Meiji Restoration.
