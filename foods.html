Food in Kyoto

| | |

Kaiseki (懐石)

A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner, often considered the pinnacle of Japanese haute cuisine. It emphasizes seasonal ingredients and exquisite presentation, with each dish carefully crafted to balance flavor, texture, and visual appeal. Kaiseki is a sophisticated dining experience rooted in Japanese culture, often enjoyed in traditional inns (ryokan) or restaurants (ryotei).

Obanzai

A traditional style of home cooking from Kyoto, Japan. It emphasizes simple, seasonal dishes, often featuring vegetables and seafood, prepared with a focus on highlighting the natural flavors of the ingredients. At least half of the ingredients must be produced or processed in Kyoto, and the dishes are known for being healthy, balanced, and deeply rooted in local culinary traditions.

Shojin Ryori(精進料理)

It is aJapanese vegetarian cuisine that originated in Buddhist temples and emphasizes mindful cooking with seasonal ingredients. It is a vegan cuisine, meaning it excludes meat, fish, eggs, and dairy products. The practice of shojin ryori is rooted in Buddhist principles, where cooking is seen as a form of spiritual practice and appreciation for nature.
